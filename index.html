Fisher-Price Laugh & Learn Stream & Learn Remote

Introduction

My name is Daegan Appel and I am a Senior in Computer Engineering at Mississippi State University. This website is used to record my dissection project for the class I chose to dissect a Fisher-Price Laugh & Learn Stream & Learn remote because it is produced by the 3rd largest toy manufactuerer in children's electronics. The remote is battery powered allowing for the reasselmby verification to not have to be plugged into any external power sources. It features 20+ songs/sounds to teach children the alphabet, colors, and counting.

Product Market and Retail Info

Product History

Fisher Price is a company that sells products marked towards children, usually educational toys. Fisher Price was created by Herman Fisher and Irving Price in 1930. They are founded on the prnciples that they created good quality products that are durable and would be good for children to play with. They continued to create children's electronics and in they have become one of the largest electronics manufactureres for young children. The Fisher-Price Laugh & Learn Stream & Learn Remote was created to help children learn the basics of counting, the ABCs, and colors. It has a few mechanical aspects to help children with their motor skills as well.

Dissassembly Notes

The pictures above show the disassembly of the product, which is fairly simple. Most of the parts come apart by unscrewing things, but when the PCB needed to be removed from the back plate teh B+ adn B- terminals on the PCB had to be desoldered from the integrated battery pack. Additionally, the platic switch from the back plate that was removed is an unused switch. It seems to have solder traces so maybe at one point it was used, but in this version of the product it has no use and the button cannot be pressed.

Product Operation

Flow Diagram for Total Operation of Product

The Flow Chart above details the workings of the entire product. There are 3 buttons that affect the output of the product. The product is in a sleep mode until a button is pressed. Each of the three buttons link to a separate Finite State Machine with four options that rotate in order. After a button is pressed the next time the button is pressed it plays the next associated voiceline that has a specific LED blinking pattern as well. The led blinks in multiple colors depending on the voiceline playing.

Human Machine Interfaces

There are three buttons that can be pressed as shown in the videos below. There are only 12 differnt sounds that I got to play from the remote even though the box states 20+. This may be due to the unused back switch or there may be rare sopunds. I personally do not believe there are rare sounds, becasue the buttons were tested thoroughly and only 12 sounds occured. The other human-machine interface are the three mechanical toys. The blue switch, red spinner, adn green spinner all move, but do not affect the electronic outcomes at all.

Product Analysis

High Level Block Diagram

The high level diagram shows the main processor is U1, which interacts with U2 the Flash Memory. It reads from the memoryu and uses a clock signal. The LED and speaker will change their outputs according to the signals sent from U1 and U1 receives inputs from the three buttons.

Detailed Schematic

This is the detailed schematic made in Kicad. Custom footprints were made for U1 adn U2 inside of Kicad. The exact number of pins needed for U1 is unknown since it is under epoxy, but since 23 pins are used it can be asssumed that it at least has 24 pins. There are some capacitor loops that only connect to U1 it seems. Additionaly, there are many traces that go to the unused switch 4 on the PCB.

Processor Details

The KV25V80066 is the part that is labeled U2 on the PCB. It is a 2.3V-3.6V Serial Multi I/O CMOS Flash Memory chip. The memory can be read and written to and it has 8 pins. The pinouts and block diagram are shown below.

The CS# on the pinout is the chip select. The SO/SIO1 is the serial data output and the WP# is the Write Protection which is active low. The GND pin is ground and the VCC is the power supply. The NC means that pin has no connection and the SCLK is the clock input for the serial communication. The SI/SIO0 is the Serial Data Input pin.

Lessons Learned

This project was unlike most projects I had completed before. It was very open ended and I had to look up how to do many things to complete this project. I learned that capacitors needed tp be desoldered to be properly measured, and I learned how to use a power supply to correctly get the operational characteristics of this PCB. I learned a new PCB schematic software to represent the PCB and I learned how to follow traces on a board to reverse engineer a PCB. I learned what jumper resistors were and I learned why they were used. I learned that some PCBs are used in multiple projects or sometimes things are not implemented on the PCB even if it has the infrastructure to support it.
